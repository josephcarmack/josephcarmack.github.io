My research experience began in my undergraduate studies in physics and mathematics. I did experimental work studying positron annihilation in materials to detect material defects. Next, I studied the solid state physics pertainig to complex magnetic materials. Computational physics and materials modeling was next. I followed this area of research into my graduate degree which is focused on computational materials science. My research experiences have burned a love of physics, mathematics, and engineering into me. However, it was not until graduate school that I found the love of my research life -- intelligence. Intelligence as a general principle and how it is implemented in the human mind. This page is dedicated to research topics that I am interested in. It includes:
  • A list of topcis I am interested in.
  • A list of groups studying those topics with links to their work and summaries of their work authored by myself.
  • Lists of research questions pertaining to my topics of interest.
  • Links to my own research projects.
